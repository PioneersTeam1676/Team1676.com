Non-Tech

Animation
The Animation Sub-Division uses Blender to create animations to submit for awards. Other projects include a Gracious Professional Video and smaller projects like animation clips for reveal videos and the Chairman’s video.

Awards
The Awards Sub-Division is responsible for evaluating award criteria and writing submissions for the Chairman’s Award, the Woodie Flowers Award, the Entrepreneurship Award, the Media & Technology Innovation Award and the Dean’s List Award.

Finance
The Finance Sub-Division is responsible for researching and writing grants, organizing fundraising totals and recruiting new sponsors. A bill of materials is updated with robot expenses, and a detailed breakdown of both revenue and expenses are logged.

Graphics
The Graphics Sub-Division creates needed graphics, images, and flyers. All logos for projects, the team’s spirit wear items, button design, and scrapbook layouts are all designed. An annual business plan is designed and created, highlighting team structure, progress, and future goals.

Photography
The Photography Sub-Division takes and manages pictures which are used in team videos, the annual scrapbook, the team's Engineering Notebook, award submissions and presentations.

Outreach
The Outreach Sub-Division is responsible for creating and managing team outreach projects. These projects include visits to local YJCCs and hospitals. Fundraising activities are organized for the Make-A-Wish Foundation of New Jersey as well as the American Cancer Society and local food drives.

Videos
The Video Sub-Division is responsible for recording all of the team’s activities and match footage on film to be used in team materials.

Mechanical

Computer Aided Design (CAD)
The Computer Aided Design (CAD) Sub-Division uses Autodesk inventor to build a computer model of the robot for the purpose of capturing every phase of the robot design, effectively helping to plan the robot’s layout and final components.

Carpentry
The Carpentry Sub-Division is responsible for building the practice field and components, as well as the robot bumpers and wooden prototype parts. The Carpentry Sub-Division builds the field that is used to calibrate the robot for competitions.

Chassis
The chassis subdivision is responsible for creating the body or “chassis” of the robot. This is what allows the robot to move around on the field.


Each year, the team assigns students within the Mechanical Division to game-specific Sub-Divisions. This year’s game-specific Mechanical Sub-Divisions are: Climber, Shooter, and BAD/Defenses.

BAD/Defenses
BAD/Defenses Sub-Division creates a mechanism to acquire the boulder and assist in defeating the opposing team’s defenses. In order to defeat the portcullis we create a mechanism that spans the length of the robot. Fins on the front of BAD would catch under the portcullis causing the door to glide up onto our BAD and across the robot.

Climber
The Climber Sub-Division constructs the mechanism of the robot which scales the tower (76in) in the last twenty seconds of the game. It needs to then lift itself above the low goal (2ft) for the points to count.

Shooter
The Shooter Sub-Division creates a mechanism to launch boulders into the opponent’s goal. The shooter utilizes two fast moving wheels to shoot into the high goal from different locations on the field.

Information Technology & Robot Control (ITRC)

Electrical/Pneumatics
The Electrical/Pneumatics Sub-Division powers all the motors with the Power Distribution Panel and control them with code from the roboRIO. Members are control the voltage distribution throughout the robot. They work hand-in-hand with both the mechanical and robot programming subteams. They are also responsible for the compression and transportation of air pressure throughout the robot using compressors, regulators, and valves to ensure the safe use of air.
Robot Programmers
Programming writes code to run all of the robot’s functions using LabVIEW. Robot control deals with both the hardware (the robot) and the software which runs on top of it. Autonomous is operated directly by the code team members create; interaction between driver/operator and the robot is controlled by them as well.
Info-Tech
Students create code for the team website. It is maintained and updated as necessary. A team scouting database is created, organized, and updated every year. This information is shared with all team members and other teams as well.
Apps
The Apps Sub-Division is responsible for creating engaging apps to share with fellow FIRST participants, as well as exciting games for all to play.

2016 Safety Animation


Safety Sub-Division

The goal of Team 1676 is to operate in a safe manner achieved through safety education, safe practices and personal responsibility.


Team Member Responsibility/Requirements
  • Take safety certification exams to be qualified to use mechanical tools.
  • Sign safety contracts to ensure they are following cautious procedures.
  • Maintain the first aid kits.
  • Participate in safety updates weekly.
  • Review safety violations and discuss ways to improve.
  • Stay organized at competitions to ensure everyone is safe during events - in the stands
Basic Safety Practices
  • Always wear safety glasses.
  • Tie back long hair - no ponytails.
  • Earplugs should be worn when using power tools.
  • Gloves should be worn when lifting a robot and when using sharp materials.
  • Do not wear loose or dangling clothing.
  • No jewelry or ties.
  • Always keep a clean and safe work environment
Machine Qualification Program
Each Pascack Pi-oneer who is mechanically involved is required to be trained and pass a test for each machine shop tool they use. Once certified, team members receive a piece of electrical tape for their safety glasses, color-coded for the test they passed.
  • Chop Saw - Red
  • Band Saw - Blue
  • Sander/Grinder - Green
  • Drill Press - Yellow
Competition Emergency Plan
Before each competition, the Pi-oneers review the Competition Emergency Plan:
  • A meeting place outside the building is chosen to provide a safe destination in the event of evacuation.
  • Every student receives “ICE” (in case of emergency) telephone numbers which are phone numbers of mentors who are supervising the competition
Attack Plan
The Pascack Pi-oneer pit crew is prepared for any emergency that may occur in the pit. Everyone is assigned a job to do in this order
  1. Take Control- This person will be telling everyone what to do when there is an emergency and make sure the area is safe to help the person who is injured.
  2. Go for Help- Pit AdMin, EMT, or Safety Advisors- This person will run to the EMT’s at the competition, pit administration, or the safety advisor
  3. Get the Team Nurse- Someone will run up to the stands and get the nurse who is the Head of an Open Wound Care Health Department.
  4. Secure the Area and Back It Up- These people will back up the situation and
  5. Perform Compressions - CPR- There is a person in charge giving compressions who is CPR/AED certified.
  6. Retrieve Bug-out-Bag- This will have the first aid kit and other essentials to any situation.
  7. Retrieve Battery Spill Kit- In case of a battery spill, this kit has baking soda and acid resistant gloves to be able to clean up the liquid acid.
